# Activity: Business Case Skeleton

## Purpose

Build the full outline of the midterm presentation. Every section. Every slide title written as a conclusion headline. Every slide with a note of the main evidence or analysis it will contain.

## Output
Business Case Skeleton v1

## Time
50 minutes

## Process

1. Step 1 (10 minutes): List all ten sections from the midterm structure. Write one placeholder conclusion headline per slide.

2. Step 2 (15 minutes): For each slide placeholder, write two to three bullet points of the evidence or analysis the slide will contain. Use the deliverables built this weekend as the evidence source.
Slide 1: Georgian Post must rapidly adopt AI to remain competitive against global logistics players
Evidence: Growth of cross-border e-commerce and pressure from international providers
Evidence: Current operational inefficiencies and slower service compared to competitors
Analysis: Risk of losing last-mile dominance without technological upgrade

Slide 2: AI-driven operations can reduce costs by >10% and improve delivery performance
Evidence: AI opportunity map (route optimization, chatbot, automation)
Evidence: Benchmark improvements in logistics industry
Analysis: Clear ROI potential and strategic necessity

3. Step 3 (10 minutes): Identify the three slides that are most analytically demanding and most important to the board. These become the priority writing assignments for the sprint.
Slide 19: Full AI transformation is the recommended strategy
Slide 28: Base case ROI analysis
Slide 29: Scenario analysis

4. Step 4 (10 minutes): Assign sections to team members. Each member owns specific sections: responsible for researching, drafting, and updating through to the midterm.
Member 1: Executive Summary + Industry Context + AI Landscape
Member 2:  Platform Dynamics + Strategic Options + Business Model
Member 3: Roadmap + ROI + Risk + Implementation

5. Step 5 (5 minutes): Identify the three biggest open questions in the presentation. These become the research priorities for the sprint.
What is the exact investment required for AI infrastructure?
How quickly can Georgian Post integrate AI into legacy systems?
What partnerships are needed to compete with global platforms?

## Midterm sections

| # | Section | Slides | What it must contain |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Executive summary | 1 to 2 | Three conclusions, recommendation, ROI headline |
| 2 | Industry and competitive context | 4 to 5 | Rogers five domains, Christensen classification, Porter, competitive dynamics |
| 3 | AI landscape and opportunity | 3 to 4 | AI adoption state, top three use cases, strategic implications |
| 4 | Platform dynamics | 2 to 3 | Platform structure, client position, threat assessment |
| 5 | Strategic options evaluation | 4 to 5 | Three options, 4-criterion matrix, recommendation |
| 6 | Business model detail | 3 to 4 | Value proposition, operating model, revenue model, AI infrastructure |
| 7 | Transformation roadmap | 2 to 3 | Three Horizons portfolio, 12-month milestones, dependencies |
| 8 | ROI and financial case | 3 to 4 | Base case, three scenarios, assumptions, Year 1 capital |
| 9 | Risk assessment | 2 | Top three risks, one mitigation per risk |
| 10 | Implementation logic | 1 to 2 | Ownership, governance, key board decisions |

## Headline standard

Topic label (not acceptable): "Industry Overview"
Conclusion headline (executive standard): "Retail banking in Georgia faces structural disruption: three fintech entrants have grown to 2.1M users with 60% lower cost structures than incumbents"
