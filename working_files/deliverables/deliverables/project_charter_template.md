## Part 1. Client Context

Describe the industry and the focal organisation or organisation type in three sentences. Be specific. A vague client context produces vague analysis.

- Industry: The logistics and parcel delivery industry in Georgia, including domestic and cross-border shipments, focusing on courier services for e-commerce.
- Geographic market: Georgia, with particular attention to urban centers (Tbilisi, Batumi, Kutaisi) and international transit corridors connecting Europe and Asia.
- Focal organisation type (e.g., incumbent retail bank, regional hospital network, last-mile logistics operator): Last-mile logistics operators and courier companies serving e-commerce sellers and buyers in Georgia.
- Why this context has meaningful disruption underway now: The rapid expansion of international e-commerce platforms (e.g., Wildberries, Temu, Ozon) is shifting deliveries to integrated platform-led logistics networks, bypassing traditional Georgian courier companies. This changes delivery economics, service standards, and control over customer data, creating strategic pressure on incumbents
- Public data availability: what sources exist for this industry? Official reports from Georgian Post, Georgian Railway, and port operators,Industry analysis from Georgian National Statistics Office and international logistics studies,E-commerce market data from platform reports and regional trade publications, News articles and press releases on platform expansions and partnerships in Georgia

## Part 2. Strategic Question

Write the one question your final recommendation will answer. This question must be:

- Specific: it names the client type and the disruption force
- Answerable: it can be resolved with data and analysis available within the semester
- Consequential: a wrong answer has material strategic and financial implications
- Contested: reasonable analysts could disagree on the answer

### Strong format

How should [focal organisation] respond to [specific disruption force] in order to [strategic objective] while managing [main constraint or risk]?

### Draft strategic question

Write three versions and select one:

- Version A: How should Georgian last-mile courier companies respond to the expansion of foreign e-commerce platforms in order to retain e-commerce shipments while managing operational and technological constraints?
- Version B: How can Georgian parcel-delivery operators adapt to platform-led logistics disruptions to increase cross-border e-commerce volume while controlling costs and integration challenges?
- Version C: What strategies should Georgian last-mile logistics providers implement to compete with integrated international e-commerce platforms while maintaining service quality and customer trust?
- Selected version and reason for selection: Version A, because Clearly identifies Georgian last-mile courier companies and the disruption force,Can be tested with available data on shipments, integration, and pricing.Strategic decisions affect revenues, customer retention, and SME partnerships.

### Why this question matters

- What happens if the client gets the answer wrong: Misallocation of resources: investing in ineffective digital or operational initiatives,Lost market share to global platforms,Missed opportunities in emerging e-commerce segments.
- What is at stake financially: Revenue losses from shrinking e-commerce parcel volume,Increased operational costs if inefficient strategies are implemented,Potential reduction in profitability due to lost high-margin cross-border shipments.
- What is at stake competitively: Reduced ability to maintain relationships with SMEs and online sellers,Loss of strategic position in Georgia’s transit and e-commerce ecosystem.

## Part 3. Analytical Scope

Define what is explicitly in scope. Be precise. Broad scope statements lead to unfocused analysis and missed assessment criteria.

### In scope

List five to eight specific areas of analysis the project will cover.

1.The impact of international e-commerce platforms  on logistics flows in Georgia
2.Cost and pricing differences between global platforms and Georgian logistics providers
3.The role of platform-controlled logistics networks and their effect on local courier companies
4.Level of digital integration (e.g., API, tracking systems) between local couriers and e-commerce platforms
5.Changes in customer expectations regarding delivery speed, reliability, and transparency
6.Competitive positioning of Georgian last-mile logistics operators
7.Operational and technological capabilities of local courier companies
8.Access to order flows and how it is controlled within platform ecosystems

### Scope boundaries

- Geographic scope: Georgia, with focus on major urban areas (Tbilisi, Batumi, Kutaisi) and cross-border e-commerce flows
- Time horizon for analysis: Primarily 2020–2025, capturing the rapid growth of e-commerce and recent platform expansion trends
- Which part of the value chain:Last-mile delivery and its integration with cross-border e-commerce fulfillment systems
- Which customer segments:individual consumers, Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) selling through online platforms
- Which competitors or entrants:International e-commerce platforms with integrated logistics, Global logistics providers (DHL, UPS, FedEx)
